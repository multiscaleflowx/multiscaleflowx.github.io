We study multiscale flow phenomena using computer simulations. This encompasses topics such as:

  • Non equilibrium fluid dynamics; kinetic theory; rarefied gas flows through channels/MEMS/porous media
  • Interfacial flows (nano droplets, nano bubbles, ice nucleation, vibrating surfaces, stability of liquid jets/films)
  • Granular flows (fundamental research on two-fluid models)
  • Non-continuum liquid flows: water flow through nanotubes; particle separation at nano pores; water flow through 2D materials (stacked graphene oxide/boron nitride etc)
  • Pedestrian and traffic modelling (a form of granular flow)

We work with computational fluid dynamics, molecular dynamics, direct simulation Monte Carlo, agent-based modelling, and hybrid multiscale methods. We both develop computational software including commercially-available solvers in OpenFOAM, as well as explore fundamental phenomena to uncover physical insight.
